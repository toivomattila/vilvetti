# Current Workflow Mapping

This section describes the current operational workflow for the target customer avatar **before the product exists**.

The goal is to map:

- who performs each step
- where information moves
- where information is lost
- where operational friction occurs

**Root problem:** completed field service information is not captured in a **structured, complete, and invoice-ready** format **at the moment work is finished**. Everything else in this map—fragmented channels, office chasing technicians, delayed invoicing, weak history—flows from that gap.

---

# Actors

## Customer

Residential customer receiving maintenance/service visit.

## Office Staff / Owner

Coordinates jobs, technicians, scheduling, invoicing, and customer communication.

In smaller companies this is often:

- owner
- spouse
- office assistant
- dispatcher/admin hybrid

## Technician

Field worker performing installation, maintenance, repair, or inspection visits.

---

# Current Workflow

## 1. Customer Requests Service

**Actor:**

- Customer
- Office staff / owner

**Channels:**

- Phone call
- Email
- Website contact form
- SMS
- WhatsApp

**Information collected:**

- Customer name
- Address
- Phone number
- Problem description
- Preferred timing

**Pain points:**

- Information incomplete
- Notes written inconsistently
- Information scattered across channels

---

## 2. Office Creates Job

**Actor:**

- Office staff / owner

**Current tools:**

- Paper notebook
- Spreadsheet
- Calendar
- WhatsApp
- Existing invoicing/accounting software
- Memory

**Information recorded:**

- Appointment time
- Technician assignment
- Customer details
- Service type

**Pain points:**

- No standardized job structure
- Job details missing
- Historical maintenance records difficult to find
- Technician context incomplete

---

## 3. Technician Receives Assignment

**Actor:**

- Technician

**Channels:**

- Phone call
- WhatsApp message
- SMS
- Shared calendar
- Printed paper

**Information transferred:**

- Address
- Customer phone number
- Problem description
- Appointment time

**Pain points:**

- Incomplete instructions
- Missing maintenance history
- Missing customer context
- Information fragmented across messages

---

## 4. Technician Performs Service Visit

**Actor:**

- Technician

**Typical actions:**

- Travel to customer location
- Inspect system
- Perform maintenance/repair
- Replace parts/materials
- Take photos
- Write notes
- Communicate with customer

**Information generated:**

- Work completed
- Parts/materials used
- Hours worked
- Photos
- Additional issues found
- Follow-up recommendations

**Pain points:**

- Notes written inconsistently
- Photos remain on technician phone
- Materials forgotten later
- Work details partially remembered
- No structured data collection

---

## 5. Technician Reports Completion

**Actor:**

- Technician
- Office staff / owner

**Channels:**

- Phone call
- WhatsApp

**Information transferred:**

- Work completed

**Actor:**

- Office staff / owner

**Current tools:**

# Major Operational Pain Points

## Operational Memory Is Fragmented

Information exists across:

- phone calls
- WhatsApp
- calendars
- spreadsheets
- paper notes
- technician memory
- photos on personal devices

---

## Office Staff Must Chase Technicians

Common follow-up questions:

- What exactly was done?
- Which parts were used?
- How many hours?
- Did customer approve?
- Where are the photos?
- Did you notice additional issues?

---

## Invoicing Is Delayed

Invoices cannot be finalized until:

- technician reporting completed
- missing information collected
- materials confirmed
- hours verified

---

## Historical Service Information Is Weak

Future technicians may not know:

- previous issues
- previous repairs
- maintenance history
- prior recommendations
- installed parts/components
